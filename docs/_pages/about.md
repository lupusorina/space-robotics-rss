RSS Space Robotics 2025 focuses on two main themes:

(1) What role does autonomy have to play in advancing space exploration capabilities and,

(2) How can researchers and engineers partner to leverage the latest developments in algorithmic robotics research on a path towards sustained development for future flight infusion?

Within the first topic area, attendees will discuss advancements enabled by breakthroughs in both autonomy and hardware developments that enable new capabilities (e.g., reconfigurable robots, new sensing and actuation paradigms, etc.). On the software front, we focus on ongoing development in autonomy and the unique challenges in the aerospace domain that call for more thorough algorithm verification and validation. On the hardware front, we will review the new capabilities that can be infused on mission concepts through advanced sensors and actuators (e.g., lidar) which have yet to percolate to the flight realm. The second topic area of discussion will be on what is colloquially known as the technology development “valley-of-death” and relate to the challenges of maturing and proving out algorithmic advancements over a long horizon of project and mission development. For example, although there are numerous advances in academic research, there are few structural or programmatic pathways for identifying the most salient and pressing capabilities and maturing them through industry or research lab technology development. RSS Space Robotics will create a unique setting that fosters discussions between stakeholders from various institutions and at different stages of technology development to identify the key gaps for spaceflight autonomy algorithms and systems.

### Invited Speakers and Panelists

1. Prof. Behcet Acikmese, University of Washington: Prof. Acikmese is a professor at the University of Washington, Seattle. He was a senior technologist at JPL, where he invented a novel real-time convex optimization based planetary landing guidance algorithm (G-FOLD) that was flight tested by JPL, which is a first demonstration of a real-time optimization algorithm for rocket guidance.

2. Prof. Soon-Jo Chung, Caltech: Prof. Chung is the Bren Professor of Control and Dynamical Systems at the California Institute of Technology. Prof. Chung's research focuses on distributed spacecraft systems, space autonomous systems, and aerospace robotics, and in particular, on the theory and application of control, estimation, learning-based control and planning, and navigation of autonomous space and air vehicles.

3. Dr. Jean-Pierre de la Croix, NASA Jet Propulsion Lab: Dr. de la Croix is the Group Leader for the Maritime and Multi-Agent Autonomy group at NASA Jet Propulsion Lab and the Principal Investigator Cooperative Autonomous Distributed Robotic Exploration (CADRE) mission, an upcoming multi-agent Lunar rover mission. His research focuses on new control techniques for large-scale robotic systems.

4. Prof. Giusy Falcone, University of Michigan: Prof. Falcone is an Assistant Professor of Aerospace Engineering and leads the Space-FALCON Lab. Her research is at the forefront of integrating autonomous systems, hypersonic and space systems, GNC, flight mechanics, and innovative uses of AI and trajectory optimization for space exploration.

5. Dr. Andrew Horchler, Astrobotic: Dr. Horchler is the Chief Research Scientist at Astrobotic, a space robotics startup focused on providing payload delivery services to the Moon. Dr. Horchler serves as the Principal Investigator for future technology development efforts and works broadly across mobile robotics, sensing, and simulation for spacecraft robotic systems.

6. Prof. Feifei Qian, University of Southern California: Prof. Qian is an Assistant Professor of Electrical Engineering. Dr. Qian’s group focuses on legged robot and animal movement, with an emphasis on analyzing and modeling the complex interactions between robots and their locomotion environments and generating innovative control and sensing strategies to improve robot mobility on challenging terrains.

7. Dr. Kazuya Yoshida, Tohoku University: Prof. Yoshida is a Full Professor of Aerospace Engineering at Tohoku University and serves as Director of Center of Robotics for Extreme and Uncertain Environments. His research interests are in space robotics, dynamics and control of space robots, space exploration technology and the development of microsatellites.
